Smash Burger

Description

Who doesn't love a good burger? Those juicy ground beef patties and melty cheese all snug inside a toasty bun...mm MMM! This recipe takes all of that and elevates it by "smashing" the patties on a searing hot griddle. This locks in the juices of the beef, and also creates a nice crispy exterior that makes the burger even more flavorful.

Ingredient

  • 1 hamburger roll, buttered and toasted
  • 4 oz ground beef, divided into two equal balls
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 slice of American cheese
  • Additional toppings/condiments as desired

Steps

  1. Prepare burger bun with toppings and have ready nearby for when burger is cooked.
  2. Preheat a large skillet or griddle over high heat. Then place balls of beef in pan and smash down with a sturdy metal spatula until patties are just wider than bun.
  3. Season patties with salt and pepper and cook until tops are beginning to turn gray (~45 seconds). Using a scraper, scrape patties from pan and flip. Immediately place a slice of cheese over one of the patties.
  4. After cheese is placed, stack the other patty on top then remove from pan and place on bun.
